Model Details

Diving into the Dataset.

We have harnessed the HAM10000 dataset as a cornerstone in our dermatological research and diagnostic endeavors. By employing advanced machine learning algorithms and image analysis techniques, we have leveraged this extensive collection of dermatoscopic images to develop cutting-edge tools for the early detection and classification of common pigmented skin lesions.

  • The dataset comprises a total of 10,015 high-resolution images.
  • Includes a wide variety of common pigmented skin lesions.
  • Expert annotations for accurate lesion classification.

Dataset Link:

https://dataverse.harvard.edu/dataset.xhtml?persistentId=doi:10.7910/DVN/DBW86T

We incorporated an extension of the HAM dataset to achieve class balancing, which allows us to provide more equitable and accurate dermatological assessments.

Custom trained CNN.

We have harnessed Convolutional Neural Networks (CNNs) extensively for image classification tasks, leveraging the pixel intensity as fundamental features. This strategic approach has allowed us to effectively classify and categorize diverse visual data by learning intricate patterns and relationships within the pixel values.

  • It has achieved an impressive accuracy rate of 90.25%.
  • The model performs well with a test loss of 0.258.

By utilizing CNNs and optimizing our approach, we've not only improved accuracy but also strengthened the model's ability to handle diverse image classification challenges, thus enhancing the reliability and efficiency of visual data analysis.

Custom Trained YOLOv8.

We have integrated the YOLOv8 classification model, a deep learning architecture based on the PyTorch framework, for skin disease identification on our website. This choice is driven by the model's exceptional accuracy in classifying dermatological images, making it well-suited for identifying complex patterns associated with skin diseases.

  • The model achieves top-1 accuracy of 80.65%.
  • Our model exhibits a top-5 accuracy rate of 97.2%.

By employing the YOLOv8 classification model on our website, we ensure robust skin disease identification with both top-1 and top-5 accuracy metrics, ultimately improving the accuracy and reliability of our diagnostic services.

Combining Predictions for Enhanced Accuracy.

We've employed majority voting and ensemble techniques to bolster the accuracy and reliability of our predictions. These methods harness the collective intelligence of multiple models, minimizing the influence of individual errors and enhancing the robustness of our system. By aggregating diverse model perspectives, we can tackle complex decision-making scenarios with greater confidence. This approach not only mitigates model bias and variance but also ensures that users receive more precise and consistent results, ultimately improving their overall experience.
